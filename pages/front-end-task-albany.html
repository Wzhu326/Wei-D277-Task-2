About Albany

History

Albany, the capital of New York State, boasts a rich historical and diverse background. Originating in 1686, Albany is one of the oldest surviving settlements from the original thirteen colonies. It was originally settled by Dutch colonists and played a pivotal role in the American Revolutionary War as a strategic location. As of 2023, the city has a population of approximatelt 97,593 residents.

Economy

The median household income in Albany in 2023 is $52,528, which is below New York State's median household income of $71,717. Geography

Situated in the eastern part of New York State, it is considered an urban centure due to its role as a state capital and its dense population. It is bordered by the Hudson River on it's east side and surrounded by rolling hills and forests on the westside. Significant landmarks in the city include:

  • The New York State Capitol Building
  • Governor Nelson A. Rockefeller Empire State Plaza
  • Albany City Hall
