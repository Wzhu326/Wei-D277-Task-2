About Sleepy Hollow

History

Sleepy Hollow, a picturesque village located in Westchester County, New York State, is renown for its history and tranquil charms. Originally known as North Tarry Town, it was founded in the 1820 by Dutch colonists; The city was later renamed to Sleepy Hollow in honor of its deep roots in American history and for its association with Washington Irving's famous story "The Legend of Sleep Hollow". As of 2023, there is a population of Sleepy Hollow is approximately 13,338 residents in the city.

Geography

The suburban town of Sleepy Hollow resides north of New York City on the wastern bank of the Hudson River. It is known for its greenery and tranqquil atmosphere. Given it's historic nature, many landmarks are tied to its past such as:

  • Sleepy Hollow Cemetery
  • Philipsburg Manor
  • Tarrytown Lighthouse
