About New York City

History

New York City, most often referred to as NYC, is the largest city in New York State and is known for its extremely diverse culture and ethnicities. Originally established in 1624 by Dutch settlers, NYC is one of the oldest cities in the United States and boasts a population of approximately 8.9 million residents. Given its great culture diversity, many immigrants travel to and reside in NYC, as per The Moia Annual Report of 2018, about 40% of the NYC population conists of immigrants.

Economy

New York City remains a giant in the economic industry today, boasting a revenue generation of $127.999 billion. The median 3-person household income in NYC is $127,000, which is greater that the New York State median household income of $71,717. Geography

Located on the southeastern tip of the state of New York, NYC is characterized by its urban waterfront locations, with multiple bridges and tunnels connecting the different boroughs; The city is surrounded by bodies of water including the the Atlantic Ocean and the Hudson River. The 5 boroughs of NYC include:

  • Manhattan
  • Bronx
  • Queens
  • Brooklyn
  • Staten Island
